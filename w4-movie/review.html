REVIEW

Reviewer: Carlos Mejia

Date: August 8, 2024

Constantine, starring Keanu Reeves, is a dark, atmospheric dive into the supernatural world. Reeves plays John Constantine, a chain-smoking, world-weary demon hunter who has literally been to hell and back. The films strength lies in its moody visuals and Reeves portrayal of a tortured anti-hero, navigating a world where angels and demons walk among us.

The film's strength lies in its rich mythology and the intriguing world it creates, blending religious themes with noir-style detective work. While it may deviate from the source material in some aspects, Constantine stands on its own as an entertaining and thought-provoking exploration of faith, redemption, and the eternal struggle between good and evil.

With its impressive visual effects, strong supporting cast, and Reeves' charismatic lead performance, Constantine offers a unique and engaging experience for both fans of the comics and newcomers to the supernatural genre.

Trivia

  • Keanu Reeves learned to perform some of Constantine's gestures by studying stage magicians.
  • The character of John Constantine was originally created as a lookalike of the musician Sting.
  • Tilda Swinton's character, Gabriel, was originally written as a male character in the script.
